---

## Overview

This site provides an introduction to **spatial thinking concepts and tools**, designed for learners in rehabilitation sciences, health equity research, and related fields.

It supports developing skills in:

- Understanding spatial patterns in health and rehabilitation access  
- Interpreting maps and geographic data  
- Applying spatial reasoning to real-world healthcare problems  
- Using GIS-informed approaches in academic and clinical contexts  

---

## Purpose

Spatial thinking is an essential skill for:

- Health services research  
- Population health and health equity work  
- Rehabilitation workforce analysis  
- Program planning and resource distribution  

This project aims to make spatial concepts **accessible, applied, and relevant** for students and professionals.

---

## Site Structure

The site includes:

- Foundational explanations of spatial thinking concepts  
- Examples relevant to healthcare and rehabilitation  
- Instructional materials designed for teaching and learning  
- Visual and conceptual tools for interpreting geographic information
